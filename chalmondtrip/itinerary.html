Yongsan / Hannam
10:00
Dragon Hill Spa — jjimjilbang + scrub ~$25
Korea’s most famous jimjilbang in Yongsan (10 min from Hongdae). ₩12,000 entry covers the full complex — hot/cold pools, mugwort steam, charcoal sauna. Add a body scrub (Italian-towel scrub, ₩20,000) for a deeply Korean experience. Plan 2—3 hours.
No tattoo restriction at Dragon Hill. Towels and shorts provided.

Michelin-listed kalguksu (knife-cut noodle soup) and mandu. The Itaewon branch has shorter queues than the Myeongdong original.

Korean-Western brunch cafe with a sunny patio. Good eggs, decent coffee. Hannam’s best casual lunch.
Tartine Hannam ~$12
Solid French-style bakery in Hannam — pastries, tartines, espresso. Good for a quick, light post-spa lunch.

afternoon — pick one
Leeum Museum of Art (Hannam) ~$22
Samsung’s private museum in Hannam — Mario Botta, Jean Nouvel, and Rem Koolhaas designed the three buildings. Best contemporary Korean art collection in the country. Never crowded.
N Seoul Tower / Namsan cable car ~$10
15-min cab from Hannam. Cable car up (₩16,000 return) or hike the steps in 25 min. Outdoor deck is free and often clearer than the observatory. Sunset panorama over the Han.
Namsan park walk Free
Skip the observatory; just walk the wooded Namsan circuit. Cool, shaded, uncrowded on weekdays. Good legs-reset after the spa.

Book ahead on Instagram or Naver (search: personal color Hongdae). An analyst drapes fabric swatches against your skin under natural light and identifies your seasonal tone: spring / summer / autumn / winter and sub-variants. Takes ~1 hour. Several studios are within 10 min of Nine Brick.
Most studios accommodate English. Book 2—3 days ahead.

09:00
Gyeongbokgung Palace in hanbok ~$25
Rent hanbok from a shop outside the east gate (₩20,000/2h, many styles). Entry is free in hanbok — otherwise ₩3,000. Go right at opening before the crowds. National Folk Museum inside is free and worth 45 min. This is the one Seoul sight that earns every tourist cliche about it.
Multiple rental shops compete on the same block. Walk the row before committing.

Bukchon Hanok Village Free
15-min walk east. Traditional tile-roofed hanok houses on steep alleys. Gahoe-ro 11-gil has the famous uphill view. Residential area — stay on tourist corridors.
National Folk Museum (inside Gyeongbokgung) Free
Skip the hanbok queue and go straight to the Folk Museum if lines are long. Three buildings, strong English labels, genuinely interesting.

Day trip — Gapyeong

~30 min from Hongdae. The trailhead starts literally at the station exit — no taxi, no bus. Bring water and sunscreen.
09:30
Achasan ridge trail Free
~4km loop, ~2 hours, easy to moderate. The ridgeline gives continuous views of the Han River bend — one of Seoul’s best panoramas and nearly unknown to tourists. Go clockwise from the station via the main summit (285m). Steep in short bursts, never technical.
Start before 10am to beat the heat. Well-marked with pictograms.
post-hike lunch — near Gwangjang-dong / east Seoul
Makguksu (cold buckwheat noodles) ~$10
The trail deposits near Gwangjang-dong, which has several cold-noodle spots. Perfect post-hike: cool, light, sour. Order bibim makguksu (spicy dressed) or mul (broth).
Sundubu jjigae (soft tofu stew) ~$10
Neighbourhood restaurants around Gwangjang-dong station do excellent sundubu. Hot, savoury, cheap.
Gwangjang Market (5 min from trail) ~$15
The trail is 10 min from Gwangjang Market. Post-hike bindaetteok and makgeolli at the central stalls. Walk in, sit down, order by pointing.

Back to Nine Brick. Long shower. The casino does not require a specific look, but smart casual is more comfortable.
